# Encyclopedia

The **Encyclopedia** is Avorion's built-in help book — a searchable, illustrated reference that
explains the game's systems from inside the game itself. It's worth knowing what's in it, because it's
the fastest in-game answer to "how does this work?" and several entries pop up automatically the first
time you encounter the thing they describe.

## Opening it

The Encyclopedia is a **tab in the Player menu** (the open-book icon). It has a **search box** and a
tree of **categories → chapters → articles** on the left; selecting an entry shows its text and
pictures (some animated, e.g. the movement diagrams) on the right.

### Contextual pop-ups

The game can also **push an article at you** when it's relevant — for example, activating
[Building knowledge](Building-knowledge) for the first time opens the *Building Knowledge* article.
These one-time pop-ups are tracked so the same one doesn't keep reappearing.

## What's covered

The Encyclopedia has twelve categories. Two of them — the **Goods Glossary** and **Production
Glossary** — are generated from the game's own data, so they list every good and factory rather than
hand-written prose.

### Basics
Controls (ship movement, camera, docking, switching ships, broadsides, cruisers), the **HUD**
(speed, hyperdrive, energy, health/shield, warnings, chat), the menus, and the starting **drone**.

### Exploration
The **Galaxy Map** (markers, indicators, rifts, search), **hyperspace jumps**, the **gate network**,
**faction areas** and **no-man's-space**, **zones** (neutral vs. hazard), every **station type**
(Shipyard, Repair/Equipment/Resource Dock, Headquarters, Research, Fighter/Turret Factory, Trading
Post, Travel Hub, Scrapyard, Smuggler's Market, Military Outpost, …), objects of interest, map symbols,
and the **characters** you'll meet — Pirates, Bounty Hunters, Persecutors, Xsotan, the Adventurer, the
Hermit, Swoks, the AI, Bottan, the Four, and the Wormhole Guardian.

### Building
**Founding a ship**, the **Building Mode** (controls, camera, placing turrets, colours, saving,
repairing), **[Building knowledge](Building-knowledge)**, **processing power**, the Steam **Workshop**,
and advanced building (transforming/replacing blocks, ship internals, turret designs, anchor/merging
blocks, ship stats, shortcuts).

### Resource Management
**Credits**, **mining**, **R-mining**, **salvaging**, **[refining](Refining)**, the seven **materials**
(Iron → Avorion), and Rift Research Data.

### Craft Management
**Crew** (professionals, officers, captains, morale, academy, cloning), **reconstruction** (towing,
kits, sites), every **subsystem** upgrade, integrity fields, shields, all the **armed** and **unarmed**
turret types, legendary turrets, auto-targeting, **fighters**, **torpedoes**, docking objects and
transport mode. *(For the mechanics behind these, see [Combat](Combat), [Weapons](Weapons) and
[Defensive systems](Defensive-systems).)*

### Fleet Management
**Captains** and their **classes** (Miners, Scavengers, Merchants, Smugglers, Explorers, Commodores,
Daredevils, Scientists, Xsotan Hunters), **strategy mode**, **autopilot**, galaxy-map orders,
**captain's commands**, founding stations, and production chains. *(See [Ship orders](Ship-orders).)*

### Diplomacy
General relations, the **relations** scale, and **negotiation**.

### Combat
**Boarding** — capturing ships instead of destroying them. *(The damage model lives on
[Combat](Combat).)*

### Trade
**Trading**, the **Trading Subsystem**, **goods** (trade/normal/special/stolen), **supply & demand**,
zones, and the trading **stations**. *(Full mechanics on [Trading and Prices](Trading-and-Prices).)*

### Goods Glossary *(generated)*
Every good, grouped into **Basic, Industrial, Military, Technological** and **Consumer** goods —
the in-game counterpart to the wiki's **[Goods](Goods)** catalog.

### Production Glossary *(generated)*
Every production building — **Factories, Mines, Collectors, Farms and Ranches** and **Consumers**
(Habitat, Biotope, Casino, Shipyard, Equipment/Repair Dock, Military Outpost, Research Station, Travel
Hub) — the in-game counterpart to **[Production](Production)**.

### Co-op Multiplayer
Co-op controls, **alliances** (members, alliance fleet, alliance vault), and **groups**.
