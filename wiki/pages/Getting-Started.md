# Getting Started

New to **Avorion**? This page is the **map of the wiki** — it walks the game's progression in the order
you'll actually meet it, and points you to the detailed page for each step. Read it top to bottom for a
sense of the whole journey, or jump to wherever you are.

> **In short:** start at the galaxy's **edge** in a small ship. Mine and trade for **credits**, fight to
> raise **reputation** and find gear, unlock **building materials** to make bigger ships, then hire
> **captains** so a fleet earns for you while you push toward the **galactic core** through the story. The
> golden rule of Avorion: **everything gets bigger, richer and deadlier the closer you get to the core.**

## The core idea: distance from the centre

Avorion's galaxy is a disc, and **how far you are from the centre is the master difficulty-and-reward
dial.** Near the rim, ships are small and weak, materials are basic, and loot is modest. Toward the core,
NPC ships balloon in size and firepower, the tech and materials climb, and the rewards multiply. Your whole
progression is a slow journey inward — see [Ship generation](Ship-generation) for exactly how that scaling
works.

## 1. Survive your first sectors

- You begin flying a small ship. Learn the basics of **damage** — enemies have a **shield** (hit first) and
  a **hull** underneath; different weapons are good against each. See **[Combat](Combat)**.
- Your weapons are **turrets**; what each type does is on **[Weapons](Weapons)**.
- Avorion's built-in help book is genuinely good — open the **[Encyclopedia](Encyclopedia)** in-game for
  controls and building tutorials.

## 2. Make your first credits

Three reliable early incomes, in roughly increasing effort:

- **Mining & salvaging** — harvest asteroids and wreckage for ore and scrap, then **[refine](Refining)** it
  into materials. Use the mining lasers on **[Weapons](Weapons)**.
- **Trading** — buy a good cheap where it's abundant and sell it where it's scarce. The whole logic is on
  **[Trading and Prices](Trading-and-Prices)**, and the commodities themselves on **[Goods](Goods)**.
- **Missions** — repeatable jobs that pay credits *and* **reputation**; combat clear-outs are the dependable
  reputation grind. See **[Missions](Missions)**.

Keep your **[reputation](Diplomacy-and-Reputation)** positive with the factions whose space you use — it
means better prices, docking and repairs, and fewer ambushes.

## 3. Build a bigger, better ship

- Bigger ships need better **materials**, and each material is gated behind a **[Building
  knowledge](Building-knowledge)** unlock — this is the backbone of progression.
- Slot **[System upgrades](System-upgrades)** into your subsystem sockets for more turret slots, energy,
  cargo, speed and jump range.
- Watch the **[Ship stats](Ship-stats)** panel as you build — it shows what Mass, speed, turning and
  Processing Power your blocks are giving you, and how to improve each.
- Improve survivability with **[Defensive systems](Defensive-systems)** (shields, point defence,
  resistances).
- Want a specific weapon? Build it at a Turret Factory (**[Turret crafting](Turret-crafting)**) or gamble
  for an upgrade at a **[Research Station](Research-Station)**.
- For heavy single-target punch, add **[Torpedoes](Torpedoes)**.

## 4. Let a fleet work for you

Once you can afford a second ship, stop doing everything by hand:

- Hire a **[Captain](Captains)** — their **[class](Captain-classes)** and **[perks](Captain-perks)** decide
  what they're best at.
- Give a captained ship a **[Fleet command](Fleet-commands)** and it mines, trades or scouts **on its own
  for hours**, even in other sectors. Automated trade routes are **[Trade Contracts](Trade-Contracts)**.
- Direct ships in your current sector with **[Ship orders](Ship-orders)**.
- Turn profit passive by founding **[Player stations](Player-stations)** — factories
  (**[Production](Production)**) and consumer stations (**[Consumer goods](Consumer-goods)**) that earn
  while you're away.

## 5. Push toward the core

- The main **[Story missions](Story-missions)** carry you inward, artifact by artifact, through the
  galaxy's barriers to the core.
- Expect tougher **[special enemies](Special-enemies)** and bigger waves as you go, plus roaming
  **[Events](Events)**.
- For prestige loot, hunt the **[World bosses](World-bosses)** or summon one with a **[Boss
  caller](Boss-callers)**.
- Handy gadgets along the way: **[Maps and charts](Maps-and-charts)**, **[Deployable
  beacons](Deployable-beacons)**, **[Reconstruction kits](Reconstruction-kits)** (revive a destroyed ship)
  and other **[Special items](Special-items)**.

## A few golden rules

- **Tech level and rarity** matter more than raw weapon *type* for damage — upgrade your gear as you move
  inward.
- **Refine where you're liked** — the refining fee shrinks with good relations and is free at your own
  faction's depot.
- **Permanently install** your best system upgrades (at an Equipment Dock) for their full effect — but only
  the rolls you're sure about, since it consumes them.
- **Don't fly everything yourself** — a captain on a fleet command is income you earn while doing something
  else.
- **Save risky jumps** — keep a **[Recall Device](Special-items)** or a [reconstruction
  site](Reconstruction-kits) in mind before picking a fight you might lose.
